Case Study: DLA Piper's Strategic Move to Cloud Post-Production on Azure

Published on: July 25, 2025

Summary

DLA Piper’s video team faced mounting challenges with its on-premises post-production infrastructure, which hindered collaboration, limited scalability, and posed data management complexities. To address these issues, we architected and implemented a comprehensive, cloud-native post-production environment on Microsoft Azure. The solution centralized the entire workflow, from archiving to 4K editing and motion design, within Azure's secure ecosystem. Despite initial challenges related to network constraints and workflow adaptation, the new pipeline has unlocked unprecedented collaborative capabilities, enhanced data security, and provided a scalable foundation for future growth. The project successfully transitioned our team to a flexible, resilient, and forward-thinking operational model.

The Challenge: Breaking Down On-Premises Silos

Before migrating to the cloud, our post-production workflow was tethered to physical workstations and portable hard-drives. This model presented several key obstacles:

  • Collaboration Friction: Sharing large project files between editors and motion designers was slow and inefficient, often relying on transferring media between physical drives or navigating slow internal network shares.
  • Lack of Flexibility: Team members needed to have physically access to the portable media source where the project existed to work. This became a significant bottleneck to business continuity when the team was traveling for work or working remotely.
  • Project Handoffs: If a team member was unexpectedly out of office or traveling for work, handing off their project was a logistical nightmare, causing significant delays and potential version control issues.
  • Data Security & Risk: The use of portable hard drives for sensitive firm-related media created an unacceptable level of risk for data loss and security breaches.
  • Inefficient Archiving and Retrieval: Completed projects and raw footage were archived on disparate physical hard drives, making retrieval for future use a time-consuming and manual process. There was no central, searchable, or scalable long-term repository.
  • Hardware Management Overhead: The constant cycle of purchasing, maintaining, and upgrading expensive Mac workstations and local storage was both costly and time-consuming for our video and IT departments.

"I was inspired to pursue a cloud-based solution years ago after seeing services like Renderro. I realized they were powerfully leveraging public cloud infrastructure like AWS, and I knew a similar model could solve our internal challenges by centralizing our resources and talent, regardless of location."

- Salvador Pantoja, Video Production Manager at DLA Piper

The Solution: A Unified Post-Production Hub in Azure

Our vision was to create a single, high-performance environment where all media, projects, and creative tools lived together. We chose Microsoft Azure for its robust infrastructure-as-a-service (IaaS) offerings, security posture, and enterprise-level support.

The architecture was designed with three core components:

  1. High-Performance Compute — Azure Virtual Desktop (AVD): We deployed a pool of four NC8as_T4_v3 virtual machines. This specific VM series was chosen for its NVIDIA T4 GPUs and vCPU/RAM balance, which are critical for accelerating video rendering and providing a smooth user experience in applications like Adobe Premiere Pro and After Effects. Each VM was equipped with a 256GB P15 Premium SSD for its operating system disk, ensuring fast boot and application load times.
  2. Centralized, Tiered Storage for Performance and Archiving: The cornerstone of our collaborative workflow is a two-tiered storage strategy that balances high performance with cost-effective, long-term retention.
    • Active Project Storage: A 10TB Azure Premium Storage account provides high-IOPS, low-latency disk performance, acting as a central, shared "SAN" for all virtual workstations. All active 4K source media, proxy files, and project files reside here, making them instantly accessible for editing by all AVD users.
    • Permanent Archival Storage: For long-term retention and initial data ingest, we implemented Azure Data Lake Storage (ADLS). This provides a virtually limitless, cost-effective storage pool for all our raw footage and completed projects. The workflow is simple and efficient: new footage is uploaded to ADLS, then moved to the Premium Storage for active work. Once a project is complete, it is moved back to ADLS for secure, permanent archiving.
  3. Seamless & Secure Connectivity: To ensure a high-fidelity, responsive remote desktop experience, we implemented HP Anyware (formerly Teradici CAS). Its PCoIP protocol is renowned for its superior color accuracy and low-latency performance, which is essential for creative work like color grading and editing. For security, all connections are routed through our mandatory corporate VPN, ensuring data remains within our company's secure network perimeter.

Implementation & Overcoming Hurdles

The transition to a completely new infrastructure and workflow was not without its challenges. Our team addressed them methodically:

  • Challenge 1: The Corporate VPN Bottleneck
    • Issue: Our mandatory corporate VPN, while secure, introduced significant latency, which occasionally impacted the fluidity of the HP Anyware stream, especially with high-resolution playback.
    • Mitigation: We are actively working with our IT security team to explore solutions like Azure ExpressRoute or optimizing the VPN gateway configuration to reduce latency while maintaining our security posture. In the meantime, we trained users on best practices for working within the existing network constraints.
  • Challenge 2: The Mac-to-PC Platform Shift
    • Issue: Our creative team has historically been Mac-based. The move to a Windows-based AVD environment required adaptation to a new operating system, keyboard shortcuts, and file path conventions.
    • Mitigation: We conducted dedicated training sessions and created shared documentation for common workflow tasks. Since our primary toolset (Adobe Creative Cloud) is cross-platform, the core application experience remained consistent, which eased the transition.
  • Challenge 3: Optimizing Performance for 4K Footage
    • Issue: We discovered that editing native 4K MXF files directly on the VMs, while possible, did not provide the seamless, real-time experience our editors required. The I/O and decoding demands were intensive.
    • Solution: We pivoted to a proxy-based workflow, a standard industry best practice. We use AVD instances to generate low-resolution proxy files from the 4K source material stored on the Premium Storage. Editors then use these lightweight proxies for a fluid, real-time editing experience. For final color grading and export, the system seamlessly links back to the high-resolution 4K source files. This optimization dramatically improved performance and user satisfaction.

Results & Key Benefits

Despite the initial hurdles, the Azure cloud pipeline has delivered transformative results:

  • Unprecedented Collaboration: Project handoffs are now instantaneous. For example, when a lead editor was out sick, I was able to log into an available AVD instance, open their exact Premiere Pro project from the shared storage, and continue their work within minutes. This level of agility was previously impossible.
  • Enhanced Security and Centralized Control: All company assets and project data remain within our secure Azure environment. Media is never downloaded to local machines, drastically reducing the risk of data leaks and providing a clear chain of custody for all intellectual property.
  • Operational Agility: We can now scale our resources on demand. If we need more editing stations for a large project, we can provision new AVD instances far more quickly and cost-effectively than purchasing and setting up physical hardware.
  • Cost as a Strategic Investment: While the monthly operational expenditure (OpEx) is a consideration, the company views it as a worthwhile investment. The cost is offset by the elimination of capital expenditure (CapEx) on hardware, reduced IT management overhead, and the immense value derived from superior security and collaborative efficiency.

Conclusion & Future Outlook

By moving our post-production pipeline to Microsoft Azure, DLA Piper has successfully modernized its creative operations. We have built a secure, scalable, and highly collaborative environment that empowers our team to produce their best work from anywhere. This project has not only solved our immediate challenges but has also future-proofed our workflow, ensuring the video team can support the firm's critical marketing and communication objectives with maximum speed, security, and efficiency, while effectively managing our growing library of digital assets for years to come.
